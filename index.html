Semi Custom Template

Using the building blocks provided, you can build your own template. Once you've chosen all the elements you would like, click "Download Wireframe" to download the wireframe information and send it to DMD.

The header will let your users know who is sending the email. It can include a logo that links to your website and a newsletter name. The header section can include a sub-header with information such as the date, department or service line, or a teaser as to what is in the newsletter ("In this issue...").

The first article in a newsletter often receives the most clicks, so including a prominent story at the top is a great way to capture readers attention. Having a large image is a great way to capture the user's interest so that they continue reading.

Additional articles allow you to share updates, conference information, new faculty, and more. Adding too many articles or too much content makes the email longer requiring more scrolling. DMD recommends a maximum of 5 articles. Your template will be created with 5 articles, if you choose not to use them all, it is easy to remove the unused ones on a send by send basis.

These sections are useful for adding additional information that doesn't fit in the article section. Upcoming conference dates, newly published articles, referral information, registering for events, USNWR badges, affiliation images, or links to popular pages on your website are just some examples that can be put in these call out sections.

All emails require certain elements be included to be incompliance with CAN-SPAM. DMD footers include all the information required as well as an extra space to add in any additional information you would like to include, such as copyright information, phone numbers, or webpage URLs.
